RAIL & SAIL

For this year's conference we would like to encourage more environmentally friendly means of travel. If you are travelling from the UK, please consider arriving to Ireland by Rail & Sail. Irish Ferries and Stena Line offer combination tickets from Holyhead in Wales to Dublin from a number of cities in the UK & Scotland.

From Dublin port take Dublin Bus (53/27/151) to the city centre to Eden Quay where you can take a Dublin Coach bus to the University or Limerick City centre.

For those travelling by Rail and Sail, some are organising a live coding train trip from UK to Limerick to live stream their performance while traveling. Follow and join the discussion

GETTING HERE

Limerick city is serviced by three airports. Fly to Shannon, Cork or Dublin airport and travel to Limerick by bus or taxi

2

Cork Airport

Cork airport is 2 hours from Limerick city. From Cork Airport take a bus to the city centre (226, 226A). From Cork Bus Station take the Bus Eireann bus to Limerick (51).

Timetables, tickets and further information available on their websites.

  • Bus Eireann
3

Dublin Airport

Ireland's largest airport is 3 hours from Limerick city centre. There are two bus services - Dublin Coach and Eireagle - from the airport that take you directly to the University and Limerick city. If travelling by Dublin Coach, note that you will change at the red cow for the bus to Limerick. Timetables, tickets and further information available on their websites.

  • Eireagle
  • Dublin Coach
